# Biometric Authentication - Quick Start Guide

## üöÄ Getting Started

### Step 1: Enable Biometric Authentication
1. Log in to your account with email and password
2. Navigate to **Settings** (gear icon in sidebar)
3. Click on the **Security** tab
4. Find the biometric authentication card
5. Click **Enable** button
6. Follow your device's biometric prompt (Face ID, Touch ID, or Fingerprint)
7. You'll see a success message once enabled

### Step 2: Login with Biometric
1. Log out of your account
2. Go to the login page
3. You'll see a new button: **"Sign in with [Face ID/Touch ID/Fingerprint]"**
4. Click the button
5. Authenticate with your biometric sensor
6. You're logged in! üéâ

### Windows
- Windows Hello (fingerprint, face, or PIN)
- Requires Edge 18+ or Chrome 67+

## ‚ùì FAQ

**Q: Is my biometric data sent to the server?**
A: No, all biometric authentication happens locally on your device.

**Q: What if I lose my device?**
A: Biometric credentials are device-specific. You can still log in with email/password on any device.

**Q: Can I use biometric on multiple devices?**
A: Currently, you need to enable biometric separately on each device.

**Q: What if biometric fails?**
A: You can always use the email/password login method as a fallback.

**Q: How do I disable biometric?**
A: Go to Settings ‚Üí Security ‚Üí Click "Disable" on the biometric card.

## üêõ Troubleshooting

**Biometric button not showing:**
- Make sure you've logged in at least once with email/password
- Check if your device has biometric hardware
- Ensure you're using a supported browser

**Authentication keeps failing:**
- Try disabling and re-enabling biometric in Settings
- Clear your browser cache
- Make sure your biometric sensor is clean and working

**Not working on mobile:**
- Ensure you're using HTTPS (not HTTP)
- Update your browser to the latest version
- Check your device's biometric settings
